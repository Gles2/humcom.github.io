Media Criticism

Course Description:

Students learn to analyze media content objectively, identifying biases and narratives. Through practical exercises and case studies, they develop skills in content analysis and apply theoretical concepts to real-world examples. Topics include the history of media criticism, ethical considerations, and the role of media critics in promoting accountability and media literacy. Upon completion, students gain the tools to critically evaluate media and contribute to informed public discourse.

Radio & TV Production

Course Description:

This course covers radio and TV production essentials for journalism. Students learn scriptwriting, audio recording, video editing, and studio operations, alongside storytelling techniques, interviewing skills, ethical considerations, and audience engagement. Through hands-on exercises, they develop proficiency in producing news reports, feature stories, and live broadcasts, while exploring the impact of new technologies and social media on broadcast journalism. Upon completion, students are ready for roles in radio and TV production within journalism.
